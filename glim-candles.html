Glim Candles

Brand Identity & Packaging

Problem

Glim came out of a single package design project. I realized there is a lack of presence in the candle industry; not many have their own retail location. I wanted to fill this void.

Solution

I came up with a candle brand, and furthered it into packaging and a retail space. This allowed for the defining of a look and feel for the identity, capturing its essence through all aspects.

Process

I defined the look and feel I wanted to create with the new logo as ethereal, elegant, and clean. The logotype is simple and clean with an airy feel, like wisps of smoke. I pulled in an accompanying serif to provide a touch of old-world elegance to the mark.

I aimed for a specific logo style for this project. While capturing the feel I wanted for the brand, I focused on a logotype-based solution. With a successful primary logo, secondary marks followed as I expanded the logo into a full branding identity.

Initial iterations were too decorative; I pushed my comfort zone to use serif typeface for accompanying text instead of sans. After refinement, the typeface was too square, and the flame too much like a water drop. The final solution had close aesthetics; I enjoyed the dot that is barely a flame.

Result

With a successful logo configured, I expanded it into a full identity; this included packaging and integration into a retail location. I designed Glim to be a boutique shop in malls or outdoor strips, that focuses on candles and the experience of creating your own ambiance through them.

I approached packaging with a unified look but subtle variances across different types of packaging. I specifically chose not to brand the flavors/scents but instead the product lines. This included hexagonal boxes containing hexagon-shaped wax melts, jars, tealight tins, and pillar candles.

To get an idea of how the packaging could come together, I prototyped through vector illustrations. This helped me visualize how the packaging could actually exist, excluding limits of material. The packaging featured illustrative prototypes that captured the ethereal and elegant brand aesthetic.
